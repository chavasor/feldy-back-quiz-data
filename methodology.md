# Feldy Back-Relaxation Reader Poll — Methodology

**Authors:** Chava Sorani, GCFP, and the Feldy editorial team
**Affiliation:** Feldy (https://feldy.me) — an online Feldenkrais Method gentle-movement program
**Data collection period:** May 2026 – June 2026
**Publication of associated article:** 2026-06-22 — [https://feldy.me/blog/why-your-back-wont-relax](https://feldy.me/blog/why-your-back-wont-relax)

## What this is

An informal reader poll among visitors who arrived at the Feldy website looking for help with lower back pain. Two main multiple-choice questions plus three single-question prompts were embedded in a short on-site quiz. Responses are anonymous; no personally identifying information was collected.

## Sample

Total respondents across the quiz: **88 people**.
Per-question sample sizes vary because respondents could exit the quiz at any point:
- Chart 1 (back-relaxation attempt): **n = 51**
- Chart 2 (treatments already tried): **n = 45**
- Summary callout statistics (stiffness on waking, back letting go, body affect): partial samples between 39 and 57

## What this dataset is *not*

This is a reader poll, not a clinical study or scientific survey. The sample is self-selected (people experiencing back pain who reached a Feldenkrais-focused website), the n is small, no demographic or clinical screening was applied, and no inferential statistics are reported. The figures are descriptive only and should be cited with that framing.

## Question wording

**Chart 1 (single-choice):** "When you try to relax your back, what happens?"
- My back stays held
- I can't tell tense from relaxed
- It actually relaxes
- I'm not sure what relaxed feels like
- I notice I'm holding, and it's uncomfortable

**Chart 2 (multiple-select):** "What treatments have you tried for your back?"
- Heat, ice, or rest
- Pills or topical creams
- Stretching or yoga
- Massage or physiotherapy
- Nothing yet
- The Feldenkrais Method

**Summary callout questions (single-choice):**
- "Do you wake up with stiffness or aches?" → 86% Yes
- "Does your back fully let go when you try to relax it?" → 82% No
- "How do you feel about your body right now?" → 74% Frustrated or apprehensive

## Why we ran it

The Feldenkrais Method is built on the premise that chronic muscular tension is held by the nervous system as a learned pattern, not by mechanical weakness or inflexibility. We wanted a snapshot of whether the people arriving at our site report that their backs respond to a direct relaxation cue, or whether they describe the kind of held-tone pattern the method is designed to address. The two main charts are intended to support that descriptive picture.
